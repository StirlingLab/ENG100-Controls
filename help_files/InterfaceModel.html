Interface Model

This interface uses a model for a DC motor with a slender rod attached. The system is implemented using the following dynamics equations. The first term is the inertial torque for both the motor and the rod. The second and third terms are the viscous and static friction torques, respectively. The fourth term is the torque due to gravity. The last term is the torque produced by the motor.

In this model are several key parameters:

  • i: The current passing through the motor (A).
  • Ki: The motor's torque constant(Nm/A). The torque the motor can produce is equal to this torque constant multiplied by the current.
  • Bs: The static friction coefficient of the motor(Nm). This friction resists the motor from rotating and must be overcome for the motor to spin.
  • Bv: The viscous friction coefficient(Nm/(rad/s)). This friction is multiplied by the rotational velocity of the motor, creating a torque that resists rotation. A faster motor rotation creates more viscous friction.
  • Jm: The rotational inertia of the motor, also called the moment of inertia (kg/m2). The rotational inertia is a mass distribution that affects the torque required to move the system. A greater rotational inertia requires a larger torque to change the motor orientation.
  • M: The mass of the arm (kg). The mass is modeled to be evenly distributed along the length of the arm.
  • L: The length of the arm(m). The arm connects to the axis of rotation (center) of the motor shaft.
  • g: Gravity is aligned in the vertical direction (up/down on the screen)(m/s2). When gravity is “on” the arm is affected by gravity. If no current is applied, the arm will rotate to hang down. When gravity is “off,” the gravity is set to zero. This scenario is similar to if gravity is pointing into the screen. The arm is not affected by gravity in this direction.
